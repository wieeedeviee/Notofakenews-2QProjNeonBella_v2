Stopping Disinformation

Help stop the reign of terror brought by fake news and disinformation!

We cannot exactly diminish fake news ourselves, nor misinformation and disinformation. But, with our little voices, hands, and ears, we can minimize its frequency or prevent ourselves from getting misinformed. Here are a few ways to prevent and help stop it:

Always research. Refrain from straightaway believing what you see. Check the news source and double-check on other reliable sources if the information is true, skewed, or untrue. Be wary of suspicious headlines and news that copies letterheads and logos of actual news for the intended spread of fake news.

Weigh in satirical news or jokes. Opinions are okay, but biased and false claims deliberately spread for selfish purposes and political propaganda can be very harmful.

In searching information in the internet, use fact-checking websites (ADL, 2021). Various media outlets and free press advocates have come to join hands to fast-check disinformation and fake news. Watch out for fake news and propaganda posted on social media that are used to touch-up blemishes and reinvent historical facts.

Do not purposely contribute to disinformation. If you already know one piece of information is unfactual, do not spread it, and especially do not paint it as the truth.

Our critical, rational, and prudent minds are our weapons against fake news, misinformation, and disinformation. Despite being covered with a curtain of lies, the truth shall always prevail.
